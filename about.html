a little about me...


I’m currently a sophomore at Brown University concentrating in [Cognitive Science], after studying [Cognitive Science in Design and Interaction] and [Interdisciplinary Computing and the Arts] at the University of California, San Diego for my first year.

Though my roots are in the fine arts, I'm interested in UX/UI and visual design, particularly the niche of ethics within these designs!



my design philosophy...


I describe myself as your everyday designer, meaning I believe design is life-based and ethically-oriented, and experiences and empathy will always blur into my work. Because of that, recreationally, I try to stay as adventurous as possible to stay inspired, and you can find me traveling, stargazing, baking, kayaking, saxophone-ing, (and even just looking forward to grocery shopping!) because those are all as important to my work as any hands-on experience. I take compassion from these experiences to then prioritize design methods that first and foremost take care of users’ optical, mental, and digital health.


Because design is so all-encompassing, a lot of the way I think about design is off-paper - the way I plate my food, the way I arrange the fridge, the way I lay out my room, right down to the last post-it I put on my desk, points to a design style (consciously and subconsciously). The blend of perfectionism and curiosity in me gravitates towards an oxymoronic style that thrives in clean chaos, elegant playfulness, and balanced asymmetry! By mixing intentional design with life’s element of surprise and unpredictabillity, I’m encaptured by the way design is inspired by the charms of flaws and discomfort.
